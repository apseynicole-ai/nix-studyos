Unit 3 Deep Pack: Scarcity, Wellbeing And Working Hours

How to use Unit 3

First pass: learn feasible set, budget line, ICs, MRS and MRT. Second pass: do wage and income/substitution examples. Third pass: redraw working-hours graphs. Final pass: answer practice without notes.

Most examinable patterns

MRS from graph, budget-line intercepts, opportunity cost of free time, MRS=MRT optimum, and wage changes with ambiguous hours.

Unit 4 Deep Pack: Strategic Interactions And Social Dilemmas

How to use Unit 4

Always work mechanically: label players, mark best responses, find Nash, then separately test Pareto and social optimum.

Most examinable patterns

Nash vs dominant strategy, prisoners’ dilemma, social dilemma, coordination/multiple Nash and Pareto comparisons.

Unit 5 Deep Pack: Institutions, Power, Fairness And Angela-Bruno

How to use Unit 5

Classify points in order: technical feasibility, biological feasibility, economic feasibility, Pareto efficiency, then fairness.

Most examinable patterns

Angela-Bruno feasible frontier, BSC, reservation IC, MRS=MRT, bargaining power and efficiency vs fairness.

Foundations Calculation / Reasoning Emergency Room

Start any Unit 1-5 theory question

Define the concept, state the model, apply to the scenario, then conclude with efficiency, fairness, incentives or measurement meaning.

Find Nash without panicking

Mark best responses one player at a time. A Nash cell has both players’ best-response marks.

Test Pareto efficiency

Ask: is there any move that makes someone better off and no one worse off? If yes, current point is not Pareto efficient.

Angela-Bruno feasibility

Check technical frontier first, then biological survival, then reservation/economic acceptance.

MRS/MRT rescue

MRS is willingness from preferences; MRT is opportunity cost from the constraint/frontier.

Income/substitution effects

Substitution follows relative price; income follows purchasing power and normal/inferior good logic.

Economic rent

Chosen option minus reservation option. Never add all alternatives.

Isocost lines

Use C=wL+rK and slope -w/r when labour is on the x-axis.

3-5 mark paragraph

Definition, graph/model label, mechanism, scenario application, conclusion.
