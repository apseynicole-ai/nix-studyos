## 1. Core Economic Models

### Supply and Demand
```
Graph requirements:
- X-axis: Quantity (Q)
- Y-axis: Price (P)
- Downward-sloping demand curve (D)
- Upward-sloping supply curve (S)
- Equilibrium: intersection point (P*, Q*)

Shifts (demand):
- Right: increase in demand (more wanted at every price)
- Left: decrease in demand

Shifts (supply):
- Right: increase in supply (more offered at every price)
- Left: decrease in supply

Movement along curve = change in own price
Shift of curve = change in any other factor
```

### Demand Curve Shifters (TRIBE)
**T**astes and preferences  
**R**elated goods prices (substitutes/complements)  
**I**ncome  
**B**uyer expectations  
**E**xtent of market (number of buyers)

### Supply Curve Shifters (ROTTEN)
**R**esource costs (input prices)  
**O**ther goods prices  
**T**echnology  
**T**axes and subsidies  
**E**xpectations of sellers  
**N**umber of sellers

## 2. Elasticity

### Price Elasticity of Demand (PED)
```
PED = % Change in Quantity Demanded / % Change in Price
```
- Elastic: |PED| > 1 (quantity responds more than price)
- Inelastic: |PED| < 1 (quantity responds less than price)
- Unit elastic: |PED| = 1
- Perfectly inelastic: |PED| = 0 (vertical demand curve)
- Perfectly elastic: |PED| = ∞ (horizontal demand curve)

**Determinants:** Substitutes available, necessity vs luxury, proportion of income, time period

### Price Elasticity of Supply (PES)
```
PES = % Change in Quantity Supplied / % Change in Price
```
- Elastic PES > 1: Producers can respond easily
- Inelastic PES < 1: Producers cannot respond quickly

### Income Elasticity of Demand (YED)
```
YED = % Change in Quantity Demanded / % Change in Income
```
- Positive YED: Normal good (demand rises with income)
- Negative YED: Inferior good (demand falls with income)
- YED > 1: Luxury good

### Cross-Price Elasticity (XED)
```
XED = % Change in Quantity Demanded of A / % Change in Price of B
```
- Positive XED: Substitutes (goods that replace each other)
- Negative XED: Complements (goods used together)

## 3. Market Structures

| Feature | Perfect Competition | Monopolistic Competition | Oligopoly | Monopoly |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------|-----------|----------|
| Number of firms | Many | Many | Few | One |
| Product | Identical | Differentiated | Similar or differentiated | Unique |
| Price control | None (price taker) | Some | Significant | High |
| Entry/exit | Free | Free | Barriers | Blocked |
| Long-run profit | Zero (economic) | Zero (economic) | Positive possible | Positive |

## 4. Market Failure Types

| Type | Description | Policy Response |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Externalities | Costs/benefits not reflected in market price | Tax (negative), subsidy (positive) |
| Public goods | Non-excludable and non-rival | Government provision |
| Information asymmetry | One party knows more than the other | Regulation, disclosure requirements |
| Market power | Monopoly or oligopoly restricts output | Competition law, regulation |

## 5. Macroeconomic Models

### Aggregate Demand (AD)
```
AD = C + I + G + (X − M)
C = Consumption
I = Investment
G = Government spending
X = Exports
M = Imports
```

AD curve: Downward-sloping (higher price level → lower AD)

AD shifters (shift the curve, not movement along it):
- Change in C, I, G, or (X − M)
- Not change in price level

### Aggregate Supply
- Short-run AS (SRAS): Upward-sloping
- Long-run AS (LRAS): Vertical at potential output

### AD-AS Equilibrium
Equilibrium where AD intersects SRAS, at a point relative to LRAS:
- Below LRAS: Recessionary gap
- At LRAS: Long-run equilibrium
- Above LRAS: Inflationary gap

## 6. GDP and National Accounts

### Expenditure Approach
```
GDP = C + I + G + (X − M)
```

### Income Approach
```
GDP = Wages + Profits + Rents + Interest + Taxes − Subsidies
```

### Nominal vs Real GDP
- Nominal GDP: At current prices
- Real GDP: Adjusted for inflation (constant prices)
- GDP deflator = (Nominal GDP / Real GDP) × 100

## 7. Inflation and Interest Rates

### Consumer Price Index (CPI)
Measures the average change in prices of a basket of consumer goods and services.

### Inflation Rate
```
Inflation = ((CPI_current − CPI_previous) / CPI_previous) × 100
```

### Fisher Effect
```
Nominal interest rate ≈ Real interest rate + Expected inflation
```

## 8. Graph Description Standards

Every graph description in an economics hub must specify:
1. Graph name and what it shows
2. X-axis: label and meaning
3. Y-axis: label and meaning
4. Each curve: name, slope direction, what determines its position
5. Equilibrium or key point: what it represents
6. Shift scenario: what event would shift which curve in which direction
7. Effect of the shift on equilibrium price and quantity

## 9. Common Errors in Economics

| Error | Correct Approach |
|-------|-----------------|
| Saying price change "shifts" demand | Price change moves along the curve; other factors shift it |
| Confusing movement along vs shift of curve | Movement = own price change; shift = anything else |
| Saying monopoly "always" earns profit | Monopoly earns supernormal profit in long run, but can make losses short run |
| Confusing nominal and real values | Always state whether a value is nominal or real |
| Omitting units from elasticity interpretation | Elasticity is unitless — do not attach $ or % to the coefficient itself |
