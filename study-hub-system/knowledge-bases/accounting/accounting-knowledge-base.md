## 1. The Accounting Equation

```
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
A = L + E
```

Every accounting topic ultimately traces back to this equation. Transactions affect at least two elements. The equation must always balance.

---

## 2. Double-Entry Rules

| Account Type | Increases with | Decreases with |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| Asset | Debit (Dr) | Credit (Cr) |
| Liability | Credit (Cr) | Debit (Dr) |
| Equity / Capital | Credit (Cr) | Debit (Dr) |
| Revenue / Income | Credit (Cr) | Debit (Dr) |
| Expense | Debit (Dr) | Credit (Cr) |

**Memory aid:** DEAD CLIC  
**D**ebit: **E**xpenses, **A**ssets, **D**ividends  
**C**redit: **L**iabilities, **I**ncome, **C**apital

Every journal entry: Total Debits = Total Credits

## 3. Journal Entry Format

```
Date       Account Name               Dr        Cr
YYYY-MM-DD [Account debited]          $X,XXX
           [Account credited]                   $X,XXX
           Narration: [Brief explanation of transaction]
```

## 4. Financial Statements — Required Elements

### Income Statement (Profit & Loss)
```
Revenue
Less: Cost of Goods Sold (if applicable)
= Gross Profit
Less: Operating Expenses
= Operating Profit (EBIT)
Less: Interest Expense
= Profit Before Tax
Less: Tax Expense
= Profit After Tax (Net Profit)
```

### Balance Sheet
```
ASSETS
  Current Assets:
    Cash
    Accounts Receivable
    Inventory
    Prepaid Expenses
  Non-Current Assets:
    Property, Plant & Equipment (at cost)
    Less: Accumulated Depreciation
    = Net Book Value
  Total Assets

LIABILITIES
  Current Liabilities:
    Accounts Payable
    Accrued Expenses
    Short-term Borrowings
  Non-Current Liabilities:
    Long-term Loans
  Total Liabilities

EQUITY
  Share Capital
  Retained Earnings
  Total Equity

Total Liabilities + Total Equity = Total Assets  ✓
```

### Statement of Cash Flows
```
Operating Activities:   [Cash from core business]
Investing Activities:   [Cash from buying/selling assets]
Financing Activities:   [Cash from debt/equity]
Net Change in Cash
Opening Cash Balance
Closing Cash Balance
```

## 5. Key Accounting Concepts

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Accrual basis | Revenue recognised when earned; expenses when incurred — not when cash moves |
| Going concern | Assume the entity will continue to operate indefinitely |
| Consistency | Same accounting methods applied period to period |
| Materiality | Only items significant enough to influence decisions need disclosure |
| Prudence | Do not overstate assets/income; do not understate liabilities/expenses |
| Matching principle | Match expenses to the period in which related revenue is recognised |

## 6. Common Adjusting Entries

| Adjustment | Debit | Credit |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Accrued revenue | Accounts Receivable | Revenue |
| Accrued expense | Expense | Accrued Liability |
| Prepaid expense (using up) | Expense | Prepaid Asset |
| Unearned revenue (earning) | Unearned Revenue | Revenue |
| Depreciation | Depreciation Expense | Accumulated Depreciation |
| Bad debt expense | Bad Debt Expense | Allowance for Doubtful Debts |

---

## 7. Depreciation Methods

| Method | Formula | Characteristic |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Straight-line | (Cost − Residual) / Useful Life | Equal expense each year |
| Diminishing balance | NBV × Rate | Higher expense in early years |
| Units of production | (Cost − Residual) / Total Units × Units Used | Expense varies with use |

## 8. Key Ratios

### Profitability
| Ratio | Formula |
|-------|---------|
| Gross profit margin | Gross Profit / Revenue × 100 |
| Net profit margin | Net Profit / Revenue × 100 |
| Return on assets (ROA) | Net Profit / Total Assets × 100 |
| Return on equity (ROE) | Net Profit / Total Equity × 100 |

### Liquidity
| Ratio | Formula | Interpretation |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| Current ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | ≥ 1 generally desirable |
| Quick ratio | (Current Assets − Inventory) / Current Liabilities | More stringent liquidity test |

### Efficiency
| Ratio | Formula |
|-------|---------|
| Asset turnover | Revenue / Total Assets |
| Inventory turnover | COGS / Average Inventory |
| Days inventory | 365 / Inventory Turnover |
| Receivables turnover | Revenue / Average Receivables |
| Days receivable | 365 / Receivables Turnover |

### Solvency
| Ratio | Formula |
|-------|---------|
| Debt ratio | Total Liabilities / Total Assets |
| Debt-to-equity | Total Liabilities / Total Equity |
| Interest coverage | EBIT / Interest Expense |

## 10. Common Errors in Accounting

| Error | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Debiting a liability increase | Liabilities increase with a credit |
| Recording cash basis instead of accrual | Revenue must be earned; expense must be incurred |
| Forgetting accumulated depreciation | Net book value ≠ cost |
| Confusing gross profit and net profit | Gross profit is before operating expenses |
| Using incorrect period for ratios | Ratios use average balances for flow items |
